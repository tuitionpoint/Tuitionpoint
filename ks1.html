KS1: A Gentle Start to Learning

Key Stage 1 is the first formal step in your child’s educational journey. Covering Years 1 and 2, this stage lays the groundwork for a lifetime of learning. At Tuition Point, we understand how important it is to make these formative years positive and inspiring. Our experienced tutors work one‑to‑one with your child or in small groups to build essential skills in a fun, engaging environment.

Our KS1 programmes are designed for children aged 5–7 who are developing early reading, writing and numeracy. Whether your little one is just starting to sound out words or needs help with number bonds and basic science, we tailor each lesson to their pace and interests. By nurturing curiosity and confidence from the start, we help young learners develop a love of education that stays with them through later key stages.

What Is Key Stage 1?

Key Stage 1 refers to the first two years of formal schooling in England, covering Year 1 and Year 2. Children are typically between five and seven years old. During these years they learn to read, write, add and subtract, and begin exploring the world around them through science and geography. The national curriculum requires schools to teach English, mathematics, science, design & technology, history, geography, art & design, music, physical education and computing.

Year 1 pupils take a phonics screening check to ensure they’re developing sound awareness. In Year 2, children are assessed by teachers and may sit tests in reading, grammar, spelling and maths. Our KS1 tutors provide gentle preparation for these assessments without creating undue stress. We focus on building enthusiasm for learning so your child feels confident when they encounter new concepts.

Our Approach to KS1 Tuition

Every child is unique, and so is our teaching. The KS1 programme at Tuition Point is designed around five key principles:

  • Play‑Infused Learning: We incorporate play, stories, crafts and movement into lessons to make learning fun. Children learn through exploration and experimentation.
  • Personalised Plans: Following our free assessment, we build a customised learning plan tailored to your child’s strengths, interests and areas for development. Our tutors adapt as your child progresses.
  • Step‑by‑Step Progress: Foundational concepts are introduced gradually and reinforced through practice. We use short, regular sessions (around 30 minutes) because evidence shows that frequent targeted teaching is highly effective.
  • Confidence & Motivation: Praise and positive reinforcement are integral to our lessons. We help children see mistakes as opportunities to learn and celebrate their achievements, big or small.
  • Parent Collaboration: We encourage parents to be part of the journey. You’ll receive regular updates, tips for practising at home and opportunities to ask questions so you feel informed and involved.

Frequently Asked Questions

What age group is KS1 suitable for?

Key Stage 1 is designed for children aged 5–7, covering Year 1 and Year 2 of primary school. It’s the first stage of formal education in England and sets the foundation for future learning.

What’s the difference between one‑to‑one and group tutoring?

One‑to‑one tutoring offers personalised attention and pacing tailored entirely to your child. Small group lessons allow children to learn alongside peers, fostering collaboration and social skills while still receiving individual support.

Do you follow the national curriculum?

Yes. Our KS1 lessons align with the national curriculum subjects such as English, maths and science. We also prepare students for the phonics screening check and Year 2 assessments.

Can my child switch between online and in‑person sessions?

Absolutely. We offer flexible scheduling and can alternate between online and face‑to‑face lessons to suit your family’s needs and location. Our tutors will ensure a seamless transition.

What if my child is shy or has special educational needs?

Our tutors are experienced in supporting a range of learning styles and needs. By building trust and rapport, we help shy children feel comfortable participating. For pupils with additional needs, we adjust our methods and collaborate closely with parents and schools to provide the right support.
